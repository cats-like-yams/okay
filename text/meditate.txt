<h2>Why Meditate?</h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Be happy: </strong>Meditation causes the pituitary gland in your brain to secrete endorphins that help elevate mood and have a positive effect on the whole body.</li>
<li><strong>Reduce stress: </strong>Endorphins also reduce stress levels in the brain.</li>
<li><strong>Focus: </strong>Through meditation you can learn self-discipline so your mind can focus with increased efficiency.</li>
<li><strong>Sleep better: </strong>Scientific studies have shown that those who meditate enhance their slow wave sleep pattern which can 
help fight insomnia. </li>    
<li><strong>Lower blood pressure: </strong>Research has also shown that meditation can reduce blood pressure, both in the short term and later in life.</li>
<li><strong>Pain relief: </strong>The long term practise of meditation can lead to physical changes in the brain that help alter the perception of pain. </li>
</ul>

<h1>What is Meditation?</h1>

<p>Meditation is one path toward transforming the mind. Buddhist meditation practices encourage and develop concentration, clarity, and emotional positivity. When you begin a meditation practice, you may learn the patterns and habits of your mind. Meditation offers you a way to cultivate new and more positive ways to approach the things you do throughout your day. Meditation can have a transformative effect and can lead to positive changes as well as a new understanding of life.</p>
